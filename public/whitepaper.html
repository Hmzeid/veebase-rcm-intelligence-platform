Executive Summary

Intelligent automation for the revenue cycle, with a human always in the loop

Veebase is an agentic-AI Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) platform that drives healthcare claims from eligibility verification through to payment posting. Twelve specialized agents collaborate over a single deterministic engine that scores every claim, applies payer-specific contract rules, and routes work to the right human at the right moment.

Unlike opaque "black-box" automation, Veebase is built on a pure, explainable rules engine: every decision produces a rationale, a confidence level, and a Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) gate. Automated processing advances only through safe, low-risk steps and stops wherever policy requires human judgment — so the platform accelerates the revenue cycle without ceding clinical, financial, or compliance control.

The platform is designed for the Egyptian healthcare context — the National Health Insurance Authority (NHIA), private third-party administrators (TPAs), and self-pay patients, with all amounts denominated in EGP and a fully bilingual (English / Arabic, RTL) interface — and integrates with existing EHR/HIS systems through native REST APIs, HL7 FHIR R4, and signed outbound webhooks.

The Problem

Revenue leakage in the Egyptian & MENA healthcare market

Healthcare providers across Egypt and the wider MENA region are absorbing the operational shock of a rapidly formalizing insurance landscape. The roll-out of the NHIA's Universal Health Insurance scheme, the growth of private TPAs, and a persistent self-pay segment have created a payer mix that is heterogeneous, rule-heavy, and unforgiving of administrative error.

⚠️

Denials & preventable rework

Claims are denied for missing prior authorization, medical-necessity gaps, coding/bundling edits, eligibility mismatches, and timely-filing lapses — each requiring costly manual appeal.

⏳

Slow cash conversion

Long accounts-receivable (AR) cycles, slow adjudication, and underpayments against contracted rates tie up working capital that providers cannot afford to lose.

🧾

Fragmented payer rules

Each payer carries its own prior-auth thresholds, filing windows, appeal windows, and reimbursement rates. Keeping staff current across all of them is error-prone at scale.

🌐

Language & documentation

Dual Arabic/English claim forms, varied hospital information systems, and inconsistent documentation make clean data capture difficult.

🛡️

Compliance exposure

Upcoding, unbundling, and phantom-billing risks demand an auditable trail and active fraud-and-abuse monitoring — not just at submission, but continuously.

👥

Scarce specialist labor

Certified coders, denial specialists, and senior billers are in short supply; their time should be spent on judgment, not on routine, repeatable steps.

The Agentic AI Solution

Twelve agents over one deterministic engine

Veebase models the revenue cycle as a pipeline of explicit stages, each owned by a specialized agent. A single engine advances a claim one stage at a time, recomputing a readiness score (how clean the claim is) and a denial-risk score after every step, and emitting a structured result that carries the agent's rationale, confidence, and HITL gate.

The claim lifecycle

Eligibility→ Prior Auth→ Charge Capture→ Coding→ Scrubbing→ Submitted→ Adjudication→ Remittance→ Paid / Denied → Appeal Written-off

Explainable by construction. The engine is a pure function: it takes a claim snapshot and returns a proposed change with a human-readable rationale and a confidence level (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW / INSUFFICIENT_DATA). Persistence, audit logging, and event dispatch are handled separately, keeping every decision deterministic, reproducible, and testable.

Governance & Compliance

Human-in-the-Loop, by design

Every agent step is tagged with one of three gates. Automated processing advances through AUTO steps and stops the instant a step requires a human — it never bypasses a required review or approval.

GateMeaningExample
AUTOSafe to advance automatically.Verified eligibility; clean scrubber pass; payment posting.
REVIEWA human must review before the claim proceeds.Missing prior auth; potential charge gap; low-confidence coding.
APPROVEA human must explicitly approve a sensitive action.Submitting a high-value claim; filing an appeal; writing off a balance.

Prohibited actions — never automated

The Phase-1 governance model hard-codes a set of actions that the engine will never perform without a human:

RuleHow it is enforced
No auto-accept of low-confidence codingRouted to a certified coder (REVIEW); AI-generated codes are never accepted automatically.
No auto write-offWrite-offs are proposed with an APPROVE gate and a managerial escalation — never executed alone.
No submitting a dirty claimAny scrubber edit holds the claim at the scrubbing stage for correction.
High-value claims flagged to the sentinelClaims above the high-value threshold are flagged to Fraud/Waste/Abuse and gated APPROVE before submission.
No auto-escalation to Level 5Medical-Director escalation requires compliance review and manual action.
No suppression of fraud flagsSentinel flags cannot be dismissed without documented compliance sign-off.

Immutable audit trail

Every claim creation, status change, agent run, escalation, and HITL action is appended to an immutable audit log — capturing the actor, role, before/after values, a risk level, tags, and the source (UI, API, engine, or webhook). The Audit Trail view supports CSV export for compliance reporting. A five-level escalation ladder routes exceptions from registration desk through to the Medical Director.

Architecture

A clean, layered, integrate-anywhere design

Veebase is a modern Next.js application (App Router, React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind v4 + shadcn/ui) that runs on the Bun runtime. Data is persisted through Prisma; the default store is SQLite, and the same code runs unchanged against a managed Postgres or MySQL database by changing a single connection string.

Integration Capabilities

Open by default — REST, FHIR R4, and webhooks

Veebase is designed to slot into an existing provider technology stack. It exposes a versioned external API secured with API keys (scoped read / write / admin), with an open bootstrap mode for first-time setup and an optional trusted server-to-server master key.

🔌

Native REST API

Create claims singly or in batch, run the agent pipeline (step or auto), update claims, and check eligibility in real time without persisting a claim.

🏥

HL7 FHIR R4

Ingest a FHIR R4 Claim resource directly from EHR/HIS systems, and read any claim back as FHIR — enabling standards-based interoperability.

📡

Signed webhooks

Subscribe to lifecycle events (created, submitted, denied, paid, written-off, escalations, agent runs). Each delivery is HMAC-SHA256 signed for authenticity.

📄

Document ingestion

PDF claims are extracted with vision-language assistance and hospital-specific templates (with a template-based fallback), turning paper into structured claims.

📘

OpenAPI & Swagger

A machine-readable OpenAPI 3.0 specification and an interactive Swagger UI make the API self-documenting and easy to explore.

❤️

Health & observability

A health/readiness probe and per-delivery webhook logs support orchestration, load balancing, and operational visibility.

Where the value comes from

  • Higher first-pass and clean-claim rates — pre-submission scrubbing and denial prediction catch errors before they reach the payer.
  • Lower denial & preventable-denial rates — proactive prior-auth checks and medical-necessity support reduce avoidable rejections.

  • Stronger net collection — automated reconciliation against contracted rates surfaces underpayments for recovery.
  • Reclaimed specialist time — coders, billers, and denial specialists focus on judgment-heavy exceptions instead of routine steps.
Phased Rollout

Earn autonomy through demonstrated reliability

Veebase is deployed in phases. The platform begins in an assistive posture — surfacing recommendations behind human gates — and progressively expands automation only as accuracy and trust are proven, with governance and audit guarantees preserved throughout.

Phase 1 · Live today

Assistive

Agents recommend; humans decide. Conservative HITL gates, all prohibited actions blocked from automation, and a complete audit trail. Builds the evidence base and staff trust.

Phase 2

Supervised automation

Proven low-risk steps run automatically with sampling-based oversight. Review gates relax selectively where accuracy is demonstrated, while approval gates remain on sensitive actions.

Phase 3

Scaled autonomy

End-to-end automation for the majority of clean claims, with humans concentrated on exceptions, appeals, and compliance. Governance, prohibited-action enforcement, and audit guarantees are retained.

Trust is the throttle. The same deterministic engine and governance model power all three phases — what changes is only how much the engine is permitted to advance without a human. Autonomy is expanded deliberately, measured against KPIs, and always reversible.

Conclusion

Automation you can audit, in the language your patients speak

The Egyptian and MENA revenue cycle does not need another opaque automation layer; it needs intelligent, explainable assistance that respects the rules of each payer, the judgment of skilled staff, and the demands of compliance. Veebase delivers exactly that: twelve specialized agents over one deterministic engine, a Human-in-the-Loop governance model that never automates a prohibited action, an immutable audit trail, and open integration through REST, FHIR R4, and signed webhooks — all in a fully bilingual, RTL-ready interface.

The result is a platform that accelerates cash conversion and reduces denials while keeping providers firmly in control — and one that grows more autonomous only as it earns the trust to do so.
